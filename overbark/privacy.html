Privacy Policy

Overbark · Last updated 10 July 2026

Overbark collects nothing — and never will.

No account, no servers, no analytics, no ads, no tracking, no third-party SDKs. Nothing about you, your voice, your dog, or your home ever leaves the phone — because we care about privacy. Yours, ours, everyone's. It's at our core: a brand promise, and a policy we're not going to change.

What the app uses, and where it stays

  • Camera. Used live, on your device, to detect the dog and place the speech bubble over its head. Frames are processed in the moment and are never recorded, saved, or transmitted.
  • Microphone. Used live, on your device, to analyse bark pitch, tonality, and tempo. Audio is processed in the moment and is never recorded, saved, or transmitted.
  • Speech recognition (optional). Only if you switch on the “Hooman Input Channel” in Settings. It uses Apple's on-device speech recognition to spot a small set of dog-directed words. It runs entirely on your device, never uses the network, and stores nothing. It's off by default, and turning it on is the only thing that ever asks for speech permission.

What we collect

Nothing. There is no account system, no cloud, and no analytics or crash-reporting service in the app. Because no data is gathered, there is nothing to store, sell, share, or delete, and no data breach is possible on our side — there is no “our side.”

Your dog's name(s)

If you enter your dogs' names, they're saved only in the app's own settings on your device (standard iOS preferences) to personalise the on-screen translations. They are not transmitted anywhere.

Children

Overbark does not collect data from anyone, including children. It requires no account and gathers no personal information.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. The “collects nothing” commitment is the whole point of the app and is not expected to change.
