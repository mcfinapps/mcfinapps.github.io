Help & How-to

Point your phone at a dog. Overbark reads the bark, the posture, and the surroundings, and floats what the moment sounds like it's saying into comic bubbles. Here's how to get the most out of it.

Getting started

Open the app and aim the camera at your dog. On first launch it asks for the camera and microphone — both are needed to see the dog and hear the barks. Give it a moment to find the dog; a reticle locks on, and bubbles start to appear. Good light and a clear view of the whole dog work best.

Reading the bubbles

Two kinds of bubble share one running transcript:

  • Barks are the hero bubbles — they appear when your dog actually barks, styled bold.
  • Thoughts are the quieter ones that drift by between barks (turn these off under Settings › Thoughts if you want barks only).

New bubbles land at the bottom and rise as the moment moves on, oldest fading out — a self-clearing conveyor. Drag the stack to put it wherever you like on screen; it stays there.

The observation deck

The bottom strip reports four live reads, plus a soundwave that lights up with every bark:

  • Mindsetthe scene it thinks you're in — walk, yard, beach, resting, and so on.
  • Insightsthe specific thing it's locked onto right now — a ball, a bowl, a door, another dog.
  • Presencehow many dogs it can see.
  • Vocalthe tone of the most recent bark.

Point two dogs at it and each gets its own column of bubbles — and they'll talk to each other.

Settings, briefly

  • Theme & Bubble Style. Seven palettes and three bubble looks, plus a Light / Dark switch that re-tones the whole app.
  • Your Dogs. Name each dog and set a temperament — calm dogs think and bark less, active ones more. It's also what lets one dog wonder aloud where the other went, by name.
  • Thoughts. The ambient inner monologue between barks — on for commentary, off for barks only.
  • Hooman Input Channel (optional). On-device speech recognition, in your device's language, so the dog reacts to what you say. Off by default.
  • Motion & Activity (optional). On-device motion so a car ride reads as a car ride, not a walk. Off by default.
  • Display. Hide the deck or the soundwave for just the camera and the bubbles.

Permissions

Camera and microphone are required. Speech and motion are optional and off until you switch them on. You can change any of these any time in iOS Settings › Overbark.

Tips & troubleshooting

  • No bubbles? Make sure the camera permission is granted, there's enough light, and the dog is reasonably framed. Give it a few seconds to acquire.
  • The reads seem off? Include the dog and a little of its surroundings in frame — Overbark uses the scene, not just the dog, to pick the moment.
  • It's for entertainment. The sensors are real; the sentence in the bubble is a very confident guess. Your dog has not confirmed any of it.
  • Works offline. Overbark never goes online — try it in Airplane Mode, it works exactly the same.
